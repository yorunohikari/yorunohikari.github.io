Me and My Background

You might be wondering, who's this guy, and why should you invest your time reading this? Allow me to introduce myself and my story.

I go by the name Osha, a devoted student of Japanese literature nestled in the landscapes of East Java, Indonesia. Embrace the truth—I'm a certified weeb. My journey into Japanese language embarked precisely when I set foot into the realm of college life. Picture this: August 2020, a clueless soul armed with meager knowledge of hiragana and katakana. A genuine dumbass, if you will.

Fast forward, and Japanese became a central focus in my academic endeavors. It's not just a subject; it's the heartbeat of my major. Yet, the language, elusive as it may be, 頭に入らなかった—didn't quite click. Surviving the initial years, I even dared to challenge the JLPT N3 exams twice, only to face crushing defeats. December brought another attempt, results still pending.

Assessing my Japanese proficiency, I'd modestly place myself at the N3 level, though perhaps that's a generous estimation. It's time for a perspective shift. No longer a self-proclaimed expert, I'll now embrace the role of an ordinary individual. Hard work and hustle are the new mantras; after all, no one is born special. Well, maybe 0.000001% of the population, but certainly not me—a normal person, traversing a realm of linguistic complexities.

In this blog post, I extend an invitation for you to witness my odyssey, a pursuit marked by the determination to achieve the coveted 合格 status in the JLPT N1. May my narrative inspire you, urging you to toil and grind alongside me. Let's set our sights on the pinnacle of achievement.

My Method to Learn Japanese

Let's be honest—learning Japanese is a genuine pain in the ass. It's not a casual stroll in the linguistic park; it's a rigorous climb up a mountain of hiragana, katakana, and the formidable kanji. Their phonology dances to a different beat than English, but thankfully, I'm Indonesian—a small blessing, considering our somewhat similar pronunciations. Yet, the journey takes a twist; enter bunpo—the labyrinth of Japanese grammar.

Oh my god indeed. Japanese boasts an array of bunpo, each more intricate than the last. It's so bewildering that distinguishing one from another feels like deciphering an ancient script. The struggle is real, my friends.

Now, you might be curious—what's my secret method for tackling this linguistic maze? Stay tuned as I unveil the unique concoction of strategies that keeps me afloat in this sea of characters and grammatical nuances. The journey is arduous, but the pursuit of language mastery is a thrilling adventure in its own right. Let me be your guide through the perplexities of learning Japanese.

  1. Immersion

    Every day, I plunge into the vibrant world of Japanese content. Anime, my daily visual escapade, takes the lead. Some folks argue that anime isn't the "real" Japanese experience, but hey, I've stumbled upon locals singing its praises. Confusing, right? Regardless, I shamelessly indulge in anime, driven more by love than linguistic purity. By the way, fun fact: I binged on 58 anime titles last year—talk about passion!

    Beyond anime, Japanese vlogs paint a lively picture of daily life. I gravitate toward those without English subtitles or, even better, with Japanese subtitles. Check out this channel that's got me hooked. She make lots of fun vlog and Japanese learning related videos.

    Oh, and I've also developed a newfound love for DIY home renovation channels—pure fun. They bought 空き家 and renovate it by themselves. I'll definitely doing that if I had enough time.

    Now, let's not forget the rhythm of Japanese music that graces my ears daily. "ムスビメ" and "Spiral," the earworms from the last season's Mushoku Tensei soundtrack, have found a cozy spot in my playlists. And Yuika? She's got the sweetest voice ever, and her simple yet touching lyrics hit differently. Go check her music.

    Immersing yourself in Japanese content isn't just entertainment—it's a subconscious memory game. Words, kanji, grammatical patterns sneak into your mind effortlessly.

  2. Daily Quiz Routine

    Recognizing the limitations of pure immersion, I acknowledge that, unlike infants, my cognitive mechanisms are no longer optimized for seamless word acquisition. As someone well into their half-twenties, the need for a more structured study regimen becomes apparent. In this pursuit, I've discovered a valuable resource—a repository of past JLPT exam papers, including a formidable 1300 N1 grammar questions.

    Acknowledging my relative weakness in grammar, I've committed to a disciplined routine—answering 10 questions daily, now on a commendable 4-day streak (my point's history). Adding a collaborative layer to this educational journey, my girlfriend assumes the role of a study partner. Her assistance ensures I remain accountable, checking my answers to prevent accidental exposure to subsequent question answer keys. To prevent counterproductive blind attempts, I've elevated my methodology. Each challenging question transforms into an educational quest, involving a meticulous lookup of unfamiliar words on Jisho, deconstruction of intricate grammatical structures, and, when feasible, the creation of educational PowerPoints explaining grammar nuances. Currently, I've shared the basics of my Japanese learning journey, and here's the deal—I'll regularly update this post with new, straightforward study methods. As I explore Japanese, I'll find easy tricks and tools, and I'll keep you in the loop with each update. It's an ongoing conversation, so check back for fresh insights into conquering Japanese without the need for complicated strategies. Stay tuned for more updates!

Updates

Update 9/1/2024

The meticulous process of crafting PowerPoint presentations proved to be labor-intensive, prompting a strategic shift in my approach. Instead, I redirected my focus towards immersive reading, accompanied by diligent note-taking within a dedicated document. Subsequently, I transcribe these insights onto this blog. The efficacy of this method is discernible through a tangible improvement in my assessment scores. I am committed to consistently updating these notes as my understanding of grammatical nuances evolves.
Furthermore, contemplations are underway regarding the integration of a dedicated section within the blog to catalog my expanding vocabulary. However, the exploration of refined and aesthetically pleasing methods for this purpose is an ongoing endeavor. On a positive note, I recently discovered a YouTube channel that adeptly elucidates complex grammar points, presented entirely in Japanese.

This concludes the latest update. Future revelations and developments in my study pursuits will be exclusively documented on this platform. Stay tuned for next updates.

Update 19/01/2024

Hey there, quick update on my daily quizzes. Unfortunately, my scores haven't seen much improvement over the past 15 days (except for a brief hiatus from December 12-14 due to attending my girlfriend's graduation ceremony – priorities, right?). Feeling a bit stuck, I decided it was time to kick things up a notch.

Enter the Anki Deck project. This time, I'm not just relying on pre-made decks; I'm building my own. Currently, I'm crafting a vocabulary Anki Deck for "天気の子" (Weathering with You) – yep, revisiting that light novel after three years since I first got my hands on it. This time, I'm committed to reading it cover to cover. Once completed, I'll make the deck available to the public, chapter by chapter. Admittedly, I've only finished Page 1 of the prologue, so don't hold your breath just yet.

That's the scoop for now. Stay tuned for more updates!

Update 22/01/2024

Today marks a significant juncture—the release of the JLPT results. The outcome is in, and to be honest, it's a miss. Just a mere 3 points shy, but hey, no biggie. I see it as a minor setback on the road to triumph. The plan? Redemption in December—I'm eyeing that N1, skipping past N3-N2. This setback served as a poignant realization: learning does make a difference. My initial attempts hovered around 68-69 points, and now, a solid 92. That's nearly half the gap closed, thanks to dedicated learning over the past 3-4 months. A reassuring testament to the impact of consistent effort.

Here's the real talk—this JLPT isn't just a test; it's my ticket out of Indonesia. Nailing N1 this year is non-negotiable, and I'm dead serious about it. Today's update may be a checkpoint, but the journey is far from over. Stay tuned for the next updates.
